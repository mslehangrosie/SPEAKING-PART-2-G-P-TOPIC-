🌟 Band 7.6+ Sample Answer:
The person who really made me care about protecting the environment is my grandfather. He lived in a small house with a big garden in the countryside. My grandfather was a very practical and gentle man. He taught me about nature not with lectures, but just by how he lived his daily life. For him, protecting the environment was simply part of being responsible and respectful. The biggest lesson was his complete lack of waste. In his home and garden, nothing was ever thrown away without a second thought. Food scraps were always collected for a compost pile at the bottom of the garden, which would later become rich soil for his vegetables. He would collect rainwater in big barrels to use for watering the plants. He even saved glass jars and paper bags to use again. I remember asking him why he bothered, and he simply said, "The earth gives us what we need. We shouldn't take more than that, and we shouldn't give it back only trash." It was a simple idea, but watching him do it every day made it feel very important. Because I loved and admired him, I slowly adopted his habits. He gave me a quiet, powerful example that protecting nature starts with simple, everyday choices.
